Galois was a prominent mathematician in the early-19th century, who is credited with popularizing the current group-theoretic perspective on field theory, the solvibility of polynomials in radicals, and (obviously) Galois theory.

He was born on the 25th of October, 1811, and died shortly after in May of 1832. He died in a duel with a artillery officer, who was involved in Galois' prior arrest due to involvement with the French revolutionary efforts.
